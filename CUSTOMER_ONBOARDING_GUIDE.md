# Customer Onboarding Guide

*For a new design partner or paying customer — what to do, in order, to
get from signing up to your first booked meeting.*

## 1. Create your account

Go to the sign-up page, enter your name, email, and a password. You'll
land straight in a guided setup — no separate "getting started" article
to hunt down.

## 2. Connect your Gmail

Click **Connect Gmail** and authorize through Google's own consent
screen. This is your real Gmail account — outreach sends from your own
address, not a shared or unknown one, and you can revoke access at any
time from your Google Account settings.

## 3. Describe who you sell to

Tell it your target industry, company size, and location (a short free-text
description of your ideal customer works too). This is the only "setup"
step that requires real thought — the more specific, the better the
prospects it finds.

## 4. Launch your campaign

One click deploys your sales workforce and starts the first stage:
finding and enriching real prospects that match what you described.

## 5. Review and approve outreach

Every email is drafted for you to read before anything sends. Read a few,
edit anything that doesn't sound right, and approve the batch. Nothing
sends without this step — there is no "autopilot send" mode.

## 6. Track replies and meetings

Once emails are sent, check back for replies (or let the automatic
background check find them for you). A reply that sounds like a meeting
request automatically shows up as a meeting on your dashboard — confirm
the time and it's booked.

## 7. Know your limits

Every account has a daily send cap, shown right on the Campaign tab —
this protects your Gmail account from looking like a spam source to
Google. If you need a higher cap for a larger campaign, a manager can
raise it directly from that same panel.

## 8. Billing

You get a real 14-day free trial with no card required. When you're
ready to keep going past the trial, go to the Billing tab, pick a plan,
and enter a payment method through Stripe's own secure checkout. You can
upgrade, downgrade, or cancel at any time from that same tab.

## 9. Get help

Use the feedback widget in the bottom-right corner of any page — bug,
feature request, or "what's stopping me" all go straight to our team,
and if you're a design partner you'll typically hear back within a few
business hours for anything blocking you.

## What "done" looks like

You should be able to look at your Campaign tab and see: prospects
found, emails sent, replies received, and meetings booked — a real
number in every one of those, not a placeholder. If any of those stay at
zero for more than a few days, that's exactly the kind of thing to flag
through the feedback widget rather than assume is normal.
